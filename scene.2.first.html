When the soldiers came, she hid her notebook beneath the roots of the walnut tree. They stormed in like a flood, taking everything she and her people held dear. They grabbed Amina-jan by the arms, dragging her away. Her last words — “Don’t let them steal your voice” — burned into her soul. In the dead of night, she dug the book out again, trembling with fear. But the fear was different now. It was the fear of losing everything, of losing the one thing that kept her alive — her knowledge. She held that notebook in her hands and knew that as long as she could read it, they couldn’t steal her voice. Even in the dark, the fire of defiance burned within her.
