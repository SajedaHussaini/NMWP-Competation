The mountains remembered the laughter of boys walking to school — and the silence of girls left behind. Laila was one of those girls. She used to dream of chalk and books, but dreams were forbidden there. The mountains watched over them as if they understood — as if they knew that while the boys played and learned, the girls like her were bound by silence. In those mountains, every stone, every tree, seemed to whisper of freedom — a freedom she was never supposed to know. But deep within her, something stirred. She wasn’t ready to stay quiet. Not yet.
