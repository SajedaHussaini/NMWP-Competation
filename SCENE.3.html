They said she was fortunate. Her husband was older, wealthier, and unkind. They called it fortune, but to her, it felt like a slow death — each day, a little more of her spirit faded away. She wore the mask of obedience, appearing as the dutiful wife, yet inside, she was breaking. Her heart was crushed with every passing day. Still, a small part of her refused to be silenced — a part that remained fiercely alive. Each night, she wrote. Every word became a quiet act of rebellion, a defiance against the life that had been chosen for her.
