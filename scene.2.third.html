She refused to run. She stood beside Amina-jan as they shouted at them, their voices filled with hate. “Why do you teach girls?” they demanded, their eyes burning with rage.

“Because a caged mind can still dream,” Amina-jan answered, standing firm and unshaken, even as they dragged her away. That night, they came for the girl too, dragging her back to her family, promising to marry her off — to take away her future. But in that moment, she knew her fight had only just begun.
