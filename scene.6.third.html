Laila did not seek recognition, nor did she desire the world to remember her personally. Her true aspiration was peace. Within the walls of her classroom, the laughter of the girls resonated once more — laughter unburdened by fear, a manifestation of freedom. As she observed their bright and hopeful faces, she felt assured that the mountains surrounding her village preserved her story. They would eternally bear witness to her journey. While the world might continue to evolve, the mountains remained steadfast, holding within them the chronicles of the past alongside the aspirations of the future.
