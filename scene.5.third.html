Laila, supported by the other girls, established a movement named Daughters of Dawn. Operating discreetly, they conducted lessons in hidden corners, vacant houses, and abandoned buildings. They shared knowledge among themselves and extended instruction to any girl they encountered who longed for freedom. Gradually, the light of education began to illuminate the darkness. Their efforts transcended mere teaching; they ignited an enduring flame of empowerment and liberation that could never be extinguished.
